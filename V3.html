⛏ Archaeological Dig Task

You are a field archaeologist on a multi-site excavation project. Over the coming weeks, your team will work four ancient dig sites — each with its own terrain and soil characteristics.

You will spend 4 days at each site, digging for buried artifacts. Use your pickaxe to excavate different areas across the site each day.

Some sites are more productive than others.

This task takes about 15–20 minutes. Please stay focused throughout.

Field Instructions

① Each day, you'll arrive at a dig site. A label in the corner tells you which site and day you're on.

② Click anywhere on the screen to swing your pickaxe and excavate that spot. Each dig attempt takes about one second.

③ You won't see results while you dig. Wait until the day ends — then you'll find out if your excavation unearthed anything.

④ You can keep digging for the full duration of the day — but only one dig attempt can be made at a time.

⑤ After each day, there's a short break before you move on.

The soil conditions at each site determine whether artifacts are present — your job is to choose the most promising spots to excavate.
